Welcome to this website about Croissants!

- a french delight

Did you know that croissants originated from a 13th-century Austrian pastry called kipferl? The iconic crescent shape symbolizes the moon, representing the victory over the Ottoman Empire during the Battle of Vienna in 1683. It wasn’t until the 19th century that croissants made their way to France, where bakers perfected the flaky texture we adore today. Authentic French croissants are crafted through a meticulous layering process of butter and dough, creating that irresistible flakiness. Enjoying a warm croissant with a café au lait is a beloved French breakfast tradition, making it the perfect start to your day!
